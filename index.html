Conclusion

Shivaji was not a revolutionary reformer but a restorer of dharma, aligning his reign with Manusmṛti’s orthodox ideals of kingship.

  • Claimed Kshatriya descent
  • Performed Vedic coronation rituals
  • Deferred to Brahmins, patronized Vedic learning
  • Protected cows, temples, and caste order
  • Governed in line with rājadharma
